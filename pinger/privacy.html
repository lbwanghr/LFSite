Privacy Policy

Your privacy is our top priority

Last Updated: January 4, 2026

Simple Summary: Pinger collects zero data. Everything stays on your device. We don't track you, we don't collect analytics, and we don't send your data anywhere.

Our Privacy Commitment

At Pinger, we believe your network monitoring data is yours and yours alone. We've designed Pinger from the ground up to respect your privacy and keep your data secure. This privacy policy explains our approach in detail.

What Data We Collect

The short answer: None.

Pinger does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information or usage data on external servers. We don't have analytics, tracking, or telemetry of any kind.

Data Storage

Local Storage Only

All data generated by Pinger is stored exclusively on your device:

  • Server Lists: The list of servers you choose to monitor is stored only on your device
  • Monitoring Records: All ping results, latency measurements, and historical data are stored locally
  • App Settings: Your preferences and configurations remain on your device
  • Export Files: When you export data (.pgr or .csv files), they're saved to your device's file system under your control

No Cloud Sync

Pinger intentionally does not use iCloud or any other cloud synchronization service. This is a deliberate privacy and security decision to ensure:

  • Your monitoring data never leaves your device
  • No risk of data leaks through cloud services
  • Complete control over your data
  • No cross-device conflicts or unexpected syncing behavior

Network Access

What Network Permissions We Need

Pinger requires network access to perform its core function—sending ping requests to the servers you specify. Specifically:

  • Local Network Access: To monitor devices on your local network (routers, computers, smart home devices, etc.)
  • Internet Access: To monitor servers on the internet (DNS servers, websites, public services, etc.)

How We Use Network Access

Pinger only accesses the network when you explicitly start monitoring. We only send ICMP ping packets to the specific servers or IP addresses you configure. We don't:

  • Make connections to our own servers (we don't have any)
  • Send your data to third parties
  • Download ads or tracking content
  • Check for analytics or telemetry

Third-Party Services

Pinger uses zero third-party services. We don't integrate with:

  • Analytics platforms
  • Crash reporting services
  • Advertising networks
  • Social media platforms
  • Cloud storage providers
  • Any other external services

Data Sharing

Since we don't collect any data, we have nothing to share. We do not sell, rent, or share your information with anyone because we simply don't have access to it.

Children's Privacy

Pinger is safe for users of all ages. Since we don't collect any data, there are no special considerations or risks for children using the app.

Your Data Rights

Because all data is stored locally on your device, you have complete control:

  • Access: All your data is accessible within the app at any time
  • Export: You can export your monitoring data as .pgr or .csv files anytime
  • Delete: You can delete individual monitoring sessions or clear all data through the app, or by deleting the app itself
  • Control: You maintain full control over your data without needing to contact us

Security

Pinger's security model is simple: your data never leaves your device, so there's no risk of server breaches, man-in-the-middle attacks on data transmission, or unauthorized access to cloud storage.

All data is protected by your device's security features, including device encryption, Face ID/Touch ID protection, and iOS/macOS sandboxing.

Technical Details

Data Persistence

Pinger uses native iOS and macOS data storage APIs to save your monitoring data. On iOS, data is stored in the app's sandbox. On macOS, data is stored in the Application Support directory. Both locations are protected by the operating system and inaccessible to other apps.

Open Source Transparency

While Pinger is not currently open source, we're committed to transparency about our privacy practices. If you have technical questions about how we handle data, please contact us.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever make changes to this privacy policy, we'll update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Any substantial changes will be announced through the App Store update notes.

However, our core privacy principle will never change: your data stays on your device, always.

Contact Us

If you have questions, concerns, or feedback about our privacy practices, please contact us at:

Email: support@lonefondness.com

We typically respond within 48 hours.

Compliance

While Pinger doesn't collect personal data, we've designed our privacy practices to align with major privacy regulations worldwide, including:

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation - European Union)
  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act - United States)
  • Apple's App Store Privacy Requirements

Since we don't collect data, most provisions of these regulations don't apply, but our privacy-first design ensures compliance by default.

Bottom Line: Pinger is a privacy-first application. We don't collect your data because we don't need it and we don't want it. Your network monitoring activities are your business, not ours.
